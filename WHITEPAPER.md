# Aequitas - A Social Network for Parents of Disabled Children

Parents of disabled children should have as much time as possible for their children. Instead, they spend a great deal of time dealing with time-consuming, administrative processes. Often 20 or more agencies are involved in the care and development of a disabled child. Each agency works differently and follows different processes and procedures, which are usually not well documented. In addition, it is often unknown what claims similar cases can make. Associations, clubs, or lawyers help selectively, but usually not holistically.

Applications and invoicing for social insurance and other agencies are tedious forms that still stem from the analog era. Mostly, these still have to be filled out and signed by hand. Although there are already some online portals from IV (Invalidity Insurance) offices or other institutions, these only help the affected parents to simplify communication with a single agency. Most communication takes place in paper form, meaning folders pile up with letters and the information cannot be used systematically.

Even if there is help from associations and clubs, parents of disabled children are often alone with the administrative hurdles and need a lot of time for them. When parents of other children sit down comfortably in the evening and relax, parents of disabled children write reports, fill out application forms, or file appeals.

## Simplifying Administration

Under the project name "Aequitas", a digital, social platform is to be created which helps persons with disabilities and their administrative representatives to reduce bureaucracy as much as possible. Specifically, the platform should help to automate routine work (such as the billing of the helplessness allowance) and thus relieve the parents. Additionally, all letters and documents should be digitized, appointments managed, and invoices checked. Expiring prescriptions and cost approvals should be automatically applied for extension.

The platform claims to be the central system for disabled people and their administrative representatives. For this purpose, an open system with open interfaces is pursued. The user interface, as well as the entire communication, should be as barrier-free as possible.

## Comparisons with Similar Cases

Another great added value of the platform is the documentation of many cases, which allows platform users to learn from other cases. This allows authority decisions (e.g. IV or public school) to be compared with other cases. This helps in assessing a decision and provides additional arguments for complaints, reconsiderations, or appeals.

This is done through analyses of reports and documentation of the cases, whereby similar cases can be found and brought together. This way, targeted suggestions can be made as to what claims still exist. Through an internal messaging system, it is also possible to get in touch anonymously with other platform participants to discuss current or future topics. Through the Privacy-by-Design approach, data protection is always guaranteed.

## Administrative Processes

* **Helplessness Allowance and Transport Billing**
* **Assistance Contributions:** The IV grants assistance contributions to help affected persons achieve independence. However, these contributions are coupled to the fact that the affected persons correctly employ an assistant according to labor law. Although there is a simplified procedure for this in Switzerland, it is still not entirely easy to do this correctly.
  * The *Assistance Module* should help to employ the relevant staff, manage employment contracts, and correctly execute payroll. Here, it should be possible to fall back on existing solutions.
* **Complaints, Reconsiderations, and Appeals**

## Recommendation System for Additional Services

In Switzerland, there are many services from many different institutions. This does not always make it easy for those affected to find the relevant services they are entitled to. By analyzing similarly situated cases and their services, recommendations can be made as to which services can still be obtained and where. This can improve the situation. The method used for this is a recommender system, which is also used by Amazon, for example.

## Legal Form and Financing

The platform is to be supported by a non-profit organization (association, cooperative, foundation). The development of the platform is financed through crowdfunding. Operations can be financed through donations, paid services, or usage fees.
